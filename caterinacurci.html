Mock Interview

People Administration Manager — Bending Spoons
Candidate: Caterina Curci Duration: 45–60 min 12 questions, 6 sections

Current Role

Administrative Assistant at Stadtwerke München GmbH (2021–present). Handles invoicing (SAP FI), order management (SAP MM), supplier liaison, meeting documentation, and department support.

Education

Master's in Linguistic, Literary & Translation Sciences (Sapienza, Rome). Post-grad specialisation in DE↔IT translation. Bachelor's in Languages & Linguistic-Cultural Mediation.

Languages

Italian (native) · German C1 · English B2/C1 · Spanish A2

Location

Based in Munich. Willing to relocate to Rome. Has lived and worked in both Italy and Germany since 2017.

Strengths for this role

  • SAP FI/MM proficiency — directly relevant to admin operations
  • Trilingual communication (DE/EN/IT) in professional contexts
  • Cross-border work experience (Italy + Germany)
  • Administrative rigour and process orientation
  • Translation background — useful for international documentation

Gaps to probe

  • No direct people-operations or HR experience
  • No managerial or team-lead experience
  • English at B2/C1 — role requires full proficiency
  • No evidence of AI tool usage or process automation beyond SAP
  • Jump from "assistant" to "manager" title needs justification
Part 1 — Motivation & Fit
1
What specifically attracted you to the People Administration Manager role at Bending Spoons?
▾
Why this question

Tests whether she has researched the company and can articulate genuine motivation. Bending Spoons has a very specific culture (high-ambition, AI-integrated, acquisition-driven). Does she understand that?

Follow-ups

  • What do you know about Bending Spoons' business model?
  • How do you feel about the expectation to spend your first months in Milan?
2
Your background is heavily administrative and customer-service oriented. How do you see yourself stepping into a managerial role focused on people operations?
▾
Why this question

Addresses the gap between her current title (Administrative Assistant) and the target role (Manager). Forces her to bridge that gap convincingly.

Follow-ups

  • Can you give an example of a time you took ownership of a process end-to-end?
  • Have you ever had to manage or coordinate other people's work, even informally?
Part 2 — Core Competencies
3
The role involves managing the full onboarding and offboarding cycle. Walk me through how you'd design an onboarding process for a new hire joining a remote-first tech company.
▾
Why this question

Tests first-principles thinking and operational planning — two things Bending Spoons explicitly values. She has no direct onboarding experience, so this reveals how she thinks under pressure.

Follow-ups

  • What would you automate first?
  • How would you measure whether your onboarding process is working?
4
You've worked extensively with SAP FI and MM. The job listing mentions "continuously improving workflows through automation and AI-enabled tools." Give me an example of a process you've improved or automated in a past role.
▾
Why this question

Links her existing SAP expertise to the role's emphasis on automation and AI. If she can't give a concrete example, it flags a potential weakness.

Follow-ups

  • How comfortable are you with learning new software tools quickly?
  • What's your experience with AI tools in a professional context?
5
This role requires managing job-offer processes end-to-end, including supporting international employees with visas. You've lived and worked in both Italy and Germany. How has that experience prepared you for handling cross-border employment logistics?
▾
Why this question

Her international experience is one of her strongest selling points. This lets her showcase it while you assess depth vs. surface familiarity.

Follow-ups

  • Have you ever dealt with visa or work-permit paperwork for yourself or others?
  • What challenges did you face navigating bureaucracy in Germany vs. Italy?
Part 3 — Soft Skills & Culture Fit
6
Bending Spoons describes itself as a "demanding environment" with an extremely high bar. Tell me about a time you were held to a standard you found difficult. How did you respond?
▾
Why this question

Directly mirrors the company's own language. Tests resilience, self-awareness, and honesty about limitations.

Follow-ups

  • What does "high standard" mean to you in practical terms?
  • How do you handle feedback that feels unfair?
7
The job listing emphasises "team spirit" — supporting the best idea, not your own. Describe a situation where you had to set aside your preferred approach in favour of someone else's.
▾
Why this question

Tests ego management and collaboration. Relevant because the role involves cross-functional work with legal, finance, and department heads.

Follow-ups

  • How do you handle disagreements with a manager or colleague?
Part 4 — Language & Communication
8
The role requires English proficiency. Let's switch to English for a moment — can you explain what your current role at Stadtwerke München involves, in English?
▾
Why this question

Her CV lists English at B2/C1. The role requires proficiency. This is a natural, low-pressure way to test spoken English without making it feel like an exam.

Follow-ups

  • Have you ever had to draft professional documents or communications in English?
  • How do you handle situations where you need to communicate complex information in a language that isn't your strongest?
Part 5 — Problem-Solving Scenarios
9
Imagine Bending Spoons acquires a company in Poland with 40 employees. You're asked to lead the people-administration side of integrating them. Where do you start?
▾
Why this question

Directly tests one of the listed responsibilities ("people side of extraordinary administrative operations including new business acquisitions"). Reveals structured thinking, compliance awareness, and prioritisation.

Follow-ups

  • What would you need to find out before day one?
  • How would you handle discovering that the acquired company's employment contracts don't meet Bending Spoons' standards?
10
You discover that a recently onboarded employee's visa is about to expire and the renewal paperwork was never filed. What do you do?
▾
Why this question

Crisis-management scenario. Tests composure, prioritisation, and accountability — does she blame others or focus on solving the problem?

Follow-ups

  • How would you prevent this from happening again?
  • Who would you involve or escalate to?
Part 6 — Closing
11
Where do you see yourself professionally in three years?
▾
Why this question

Bending Spoons emphasises rapid career growth. Tests ambition and whether her trajectory aligns with the company's expectations.

Follow-ups

  • What specific skills do you want to develop in this role?
12
Is there anything about your background that you feel we haven't covered, or anything you'd like to ask me about the role?
▾
Why this question

Standard closer, but revealing. Strong candidates ask informed questions about the company, the team, or the role's scope.
